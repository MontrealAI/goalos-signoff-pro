# Acceptance workflow

GoalOS Signoff Pro exists because AI work is not accepted merely because it was produced. Acceptance requires criteria, evidence, review, human authority, and a signed/replayable receipt.

## Six-gate workflow

1. **Commission work** — define the objective, roles, acceptance criteria, risk boundaries, and required evidence.
2. **Submit evidence** — assemble artifacts, reports, logs, or public-safe summaries into an Evidence Docket.
3. **Map evidence to criteria** — connect each criterion to supporting evidence and mark gaps or contradictions.
4. **Review** — reviewers inspect claims, evidence, replay readiness, risks, and unresolved disputes.
5. **Human decision** — an authorized person accepts, rejects, or requests changes.
6. **Signed Mission Receipt and verification** — accepted work receives a receipt that can be checked against hashes, evidence, replay notes, and claim boundaries.

## Decision states

| State | Meaning | Required posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Draft | Work is being prepared. | No strong public claim. |
| Submitted | Evidence is available for review. | Docket must identify gaps. |
| Changes requested | Review found missing or conflicting evidence. | Do not promote the claim. |
| Rejected | Acceptance criteria were not met. | Preserve the record for audit trail. |
| Accepted | Human authority approved bounded acceptance. | Issue and verify a Mission Receipt. |

## Public-safe demo standard

Public pages may demonstrate the workflow with sample artifacts and local verification. They must not collect user data, accept uploads, submit public forms, connect wallets, move value, create payments, or grant production authority.

## Cross-suite relationship

Signoff Pro answers: **Can this AI-delivered work be accepted?** GoalOS AGIJobManager Ascension answers: **Can autonomous work be proven, replayed, validated, remembered, and made settlement-ready without public unsafe action?** Cross-links between the repositories are explanatory only and do not imply shared custody, live settlement, or production authority.
