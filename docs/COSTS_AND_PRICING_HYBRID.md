# Costs and unit economics

## Phase A: SaaS Basic

Costs are ordinary SaaS costs:

- hosting
- database
- storage
- email
- AI-assisted mechanical checks, if enabled
- support

No gas, no AGIALPHA, no wallet support burden.

## Phase B: Verified anchoring

Additional cost per anchored receipt:

- Ethereum gas, paid by relayer or customer wallet
- RPC calls
- explorer verification
- optional IPFS pinning

Practical pricing:

- Basic receipt included in subscription
- Verified receipt as an add-on, e.g. $5–$25 depending on gas and support
- Human review as a higher-price add-on

## Phase C: AGIALPHA-secured review

AGIALPHA is used as refundable collateral or protocol fee. It should not replace normal customer pricing.

Recommended split:

- customer pays subscription / review fee in normal money
- GoalOS handles gas and UX
- reviewers receive ordinary money or later protocol rewards
- AGIALPHA collateral secures reviewer behavior

## Unit economics rule

Never price production based only on gas. The true cost includes support, disputes, storage, incident response, compliance, reviewer operations, and security monitoring.
