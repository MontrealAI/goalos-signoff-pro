# GoalOS Signoff Hybrid: absolute best first commercial product

The best product is neither pure SaaS nor pure Web3. It is a **trust-minimized SaaS with progressive Web3 guarantees**.

## Core promise

> Know when AI work is actually done, and produce a receipt that can be verified later.

## Why hybrid wins

Pure SaaS is easiest to sell, but the customer must trust the database forever. Pure Web3 is portable and verifiable, but most mainstream buyers do not want wallets, gas, IPFS, or contract interactions.

The hybrid product gives both:

- SaaS UX for mainstream customers.
- Cryptographic file fingerprints for integrity.
- Digital signatures for immediate verification.
- Optional Ethereum anchoring for public timestamp/tamper evidence.
- Optional IPFS publication for public/redacted evidence metadata.
- Future AGIALPHA bonds and settlement after protocol readiness.

## Customer-facing offer

**GoalOS Signoff**

- Define what “done” means.
- Submit the work and evidence.
- Review against acceptance criteria.
- Accept, request changes, or reject.
- Issue a Mission Receipt.
- Optionally make the receipt publicly verifiable on Ethereum.

## First paying users

1. AI consultants delivering client reports and workflows.
2. Small AI agencies managing handoff and acceptance.
3. Enterprise AI pilot teams documenting whether a pilot passed.
4. DAO/grant programs that need transparent milestone acceptance.
5. Web3 freelancers who want on-chain proof of accepted work.

## What not to sell first

Do not sell public escrow, AGIALPHA staking, or autonomous settlement as the first mainstream product. Those are powerful later upgrades, but premature activation creates unnecessary security, custody, audit, and legal risk.
