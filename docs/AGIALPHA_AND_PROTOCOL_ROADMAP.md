# AGIALPHA and 48-Contract Protocol Roadmap

This hybrid product is designed to grow into the existing GoalOS protocol without forcing crypto on day-one customers.

## Stage 1 — Commercial Signoff

- No wallet required.
- No AGIALPHA required.
- No escrow.
- Signed Mission Receipts create demand for the acceptance workflow.

## Stage 2 — Verified Receipts

- Optional on-chain hash anchors.
- No user funds.
- No AGIALPHA bonds.
- Creates externally verifiable proof of acceptance.

## Stage 3 — AGIALPHA-Secured Review

After audit and activation, AGIALPHA can secure reviewer and builder behavior:

- reviewer bonds;
- builder proof bonds;
- challenge stakes;
- proof-card and credential fees;
- reputation-linked participation.

## Stage 4 — Proof-to-Payment

After explicit user-fund authorization, escrow can be added:

- client funds a reward;
- builder submits evidence;
- reviewer and client approve;
- payment releases according to the rules;
- disputes suspend settlement.

## Principle

Stable money should pay for work. AGIALPHA should secure accountability. ETH pays the network. GoalOS earns SaaS, review, verification and protocol fees.
