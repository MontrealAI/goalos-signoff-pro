> The first buyers, anti-personas, and selection criteria.

---

## Best initial customer profile

A small AI consultancy or agency delivering evidence-heavy work to clients.

## Why this segment

- They already sell AI-assisted output
- They face client acceptance ambiguity
- They can adopt tools quickly
- They benefit from professionalized delivery
- They can pay modest SaaS fees
- They create repeat workflows

## Ideal pilot customer scorecard

| Criterion | Strong signal | Weak signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Volume | At least 3 deliverables/month | One-off experiment |
| Evidence need | Sources, screenshots, tests, files | Purely subjective creative work |
| Approval pain | Repeated client ambiguity | No approval process |
| Buyer authority | Founder, operator, agency lead | No decision-maker |
| Trust sensitivity | Needs audit trail | Does not care about records |
| Web3 tolerance | Interested but not dependent | Requires Mainnet settlement now |


## Strong initial niches

- AI strategy reports
- AI automation handoff
- Software feature delivery
- Grant/milestone evidence
- Content campaign acceptance
- Procurement or vendor pilots

## Anti-personas

- Users who want unrestricted escrow immediately
- Customers requiring regulated custody
- Consumers with one-time low-value tasks
- Teams unwilling to define acceptance criteria
- Customers who require guaranteed factual truth from AI

## Customer interview must-ask questions

- [ ] How do you currently know a deliverable is accepted?
- [ ] When did acceptance ambiguity last cause a problem?
- [ ] Who approves work today?
- [ ] What evidence do clients ask for?
- [ ] Would a signed receipt help you get paid faster?
- [ ] Would optional public verification help or scare your customers?
