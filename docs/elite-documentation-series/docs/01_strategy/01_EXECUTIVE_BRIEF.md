## One sentence

**GoalOS Signoff Pro helps teams define done, collect evidence, obtain human acceptance, and issue signed Mission Receipts for AI-assisted work — with optional public verification when trust matters.**

## Why now

AI work is becoming easier to generate and harder to accept responsibly. Teams need a clear record of:

- what was requested
- what was delivered
- which evidence supported it
- who accepted it
- what limitations remained
- which version was approved

Email, Slack, shared drives, and ordinary invoices do not create a strong acceptance record. GoalOS Signoff fills that gap.

## Product thesis

The winning first product is not pure Web3 and not pure SaaS. It is hybrid:

| Layer | Customer experience | Strategic value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mainstream SaaS | No wallet, no token, no gas, no crypto confusion | Adoption and revenue |
| Signed receipts | Downloadable, shareable, verifiable acceptance records | Trust artifact |
| Optional IPFS/Ethereum anchoring | Only for customers who need public verification | Protocol bridge |
| Future `$AGIALPHA` rails | Bonded review, disputes, proof-to-payment | Long-term defensibility |


## Initial customer

AI consultants, small AI agencies, software contractors, research teams, and enterprise AI pilot leads.

## Initial use case

A client or manager accepts an AI-assisted deliverable only after checking a structured evidence page.

## Business model

| Plan | Initial offer | Reason |

| Free pilot | 3 Signoffs/month | Remove adoption friction |
| Professional | ~$49/month | Individual consultants |
| Team | ~$199–$299/month | Agencies and teams |

## The one metric that matters first

**Repeat intent after a completed Signoff.** If users do not want to reuse it for real work, blockchain anchoring and tokenomics will not rescue the product.

## 90-day outcome

By day 90, leadership should know:

- [ ] Which customer segment completed Signoffs fastest
- [ ] Which templates produced repeat intent
- [ ] Whether verified receipts increased trust or caused confusion
- [ ] Whether users would pay for team collaboration
- [ ] What must be fixed before public launch
