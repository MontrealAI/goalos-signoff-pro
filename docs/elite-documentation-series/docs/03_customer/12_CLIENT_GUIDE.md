## What you do

You define what you want, review the evidence, and decide whether to accept.

## Step-by-step

- [ ] Choose a template
- [ ] Write the assignment in plain language
- [ ] Confirm acceptance criteria
- [ ] Invite the builder
- [ ] Wait for submission
- [ ] Review evidence assistant warnings
- [ ] Read reviewer notes if present
- [ ] Accept, request changes, or reject
- [ ] Download the Mission Receipt

## Good acceptance criteria examples

- The report covers at least five competitors
- Every major claim has a source link
- The automation includes a rollback plan
- The feature includes screenshots and passing tests
- Limitations and uncertainties are disclosed

## Bad acceptance criteria examples

- Make it good
- Do research
- Use AI well
- Be comprehensive
- Optimize our business

## What acceptance means

Acceptance means you approve the specific version shown on the receipt. It does not automatically mean you waive legal rights, pay an invoice, or certify that every statement is true unless your separate contract says so.
