# Absolute best product specification

## Product name

GoalOS Signoff Hybrid

## Core promise

Know when AI-assisted work is actually done, and make the accepted result independently verifiable.

## Customer-facing workflow

1. Client defines the brief and acceptance criteria.
2. Builder submits the work and supporting evidence.
3. GoalOS performs mechanical completeness and integrity checks.
4. Reviewer recommends accept, changes, or reject.
5. Client makes the final decision.
6. GoalOS issues a signed Mission Receipt.
7. Optional: GoalOS anchors the receipt hash on-chain and adds a verification badge.

## Why hybrid wins

- Traditional SaaS gives mainstream onboarding, private files, account recovery, collaboration, email, billing, and support.
- Blockchain gives public verifiability, tamper-evident receipt anchoring, portable evidence hashes, and a path to AGIALPHA-secured review and settlement.
- The customer does not need to understand either side. They see one clear workflow and one clear result.

## First production mode

Launch with:

- Standard Signoff enabled.
- Verified Signoff enabled only on Sepolia or private deployments.
- Mainnet anchoring disabled until operator signoff.
- AGIALPHA fees disabled.
- Bonded review disabled.
- Escrow disabled.

## Recommended commercial pricing

- Free: 3 Standard Signoffs per month.
- Professional: $49/month.
- Team: $249/month.
- Verified receipt add-on: $5–$15 per anchored receipt, or included in Team.
- Human review: $99–$299 per project.

## Metrics that matter

- Signoffs attempted.
- Signoffs completed.
- Receipts shared externally.
- Time from brief to acceptance.
- Number of disputes prevented or clarified.
- Percentage of customers who request Verified Signoff.

## What not to sell first

- Escrow.
- Token staking.
- AGIALPHA investment claims.
- Autonomous settlement.
- Mainnet protocol economy.

Those are later phases after demand, audit, and operational readiness are proven.
