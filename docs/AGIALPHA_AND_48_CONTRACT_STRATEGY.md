# AGIALPHA and 48-contract strategy

## Honest status

The current 48-contract GoalOS deployment is valuable protocol infrastructure, but it should not be forced into the first mainstream SaaS workflow until the readiness gates are complete.

The hybrid product uses the protocol concepts now and activates the token economy later.

## How this product leverages the protocol now

The SaaS receipt model is structured around the same primitives:

- mission commitment
- evidence docket
- claim boundary
- risk ledger
- review decision
- proof bundle
- receipt / credential
- reputation-ready identity

The package exports hashes compatible with later protocol registration.

## How AGIALPHA becomes useful later

AGIALPHA should secure behavior, not block onboarding.

Future roles:

1. reviewer bonds
2. builder bonds
3. challenge stakes
4. proof-card and credential fees
5. reputation-weighted reviewer markets
6. proof-to-payment settlement after user-fund authorization

## Launch order

```text
1. SaaS Signoff receipts
2. Optional Ethereum anchoring
3. Optional wallet-native Signoff
4. AGIALPHA-secured reviewer bonds on testnet
5. Controlled Mainnet canary after audit
6. Proof-to-payment only after user-fund authorization
```

## Customer messaging

Do not tell mainstream customers they need AGIALPHA.

Tell them:

> “GoalOS gives you a clear acceptance record. Advanced verified and secured modes are available when your organization wants public verification or bonded review.”
