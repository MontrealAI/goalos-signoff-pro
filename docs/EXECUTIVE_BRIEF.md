# Executive brief — GoalOS Signoff Pro

GoalOS Signoff Pro is the institutional acceptance layer for AI-delivered work: mission briefs, evidence, criteria mapping, human review, governed decisions, and signed Mission Receipts.

## The two-minute value

AI work is not accepted just because a model produced it. Teams need a repeatable way to ask: what was commissioned, what evidence exists, which criteria were satisfied, who reviewed the record, what decision was made, and how can the receipt be verified later?

GoalOS Signoff Pro turns that acceptance question into a proof loop:

1. Commission the work with a mission brief and acceptance criteria.
2. Collect or reference evidence in an Evidence Docket.
3. Map evidence to criteria and unresolved gaps.
4. Review the claim with human authority.
5. Decide: accept, reject, or request changes.
6. Issue a signed/replayable Mission Receipt when accepted.

## Why it matters

- Executives get a calm proof-to-acceptance story instead of a black-box AI output story.
- Reviewers get Evidence Dockets, public-safe artifacts, route manifests, and verifiers.
- Developers get deterministic local checks and generated-site conventions.
- Operators get explicit boundaries for public demos, optional anchoring, and private-beta surfaces.
- Legal/risk readers get plain-language claim boundaries and no-wallet/no-data public posture.

## Public-safe posture

The public site demonstrates proof logic. It does not move funds, connect wallets, request token approvals, switch networks, broadcast transactions, collect personal data, accept uploads, run analytics, set cookies, or grant production authority.

AGIALPHA and settlement references remain identity, protocol-boundary, simulation, or optional expert-only references unless a future human-authority-gated document states otherwise.

## Cross-suite relationship

GoalOS Signoff Pro and GoalOS AGIJobManager Ascension are complementary public-safe GoalOS surfaces. Signoff Pro focuses on acceptance: briefs, evidence, review, human authority, signed receipts, and verification. AGIJobManager Ascension focuses on proof-settlement logic: work requests, proof bundles, replay, validation, settlement-readiness signals, and Chronicle memory. Together they demonstrate how autonomous AI work can become bounded, reviewable, replayable, and institutionally governed.

| Surface | Primary question | Core object | Public posture |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Signoff Pro | Can this work be accepted? | Mission Receipt / Evidence Docket | Browser-local/public-safe; no wallet; no data |
| AGIJobManager Ascension | Can this work be settled or remembered? | ProofBundle / SettlementReceipt / Chronicle entry | Browser-local/read-only simulation; no funds moved |

Cross-links do not imply live shared custody, live settlement, token availability, or production authority.

## What would prove more

Stronger public claims would require real missions, public Evidence Dockets, replay logs, validator reports, cost/risk ledgers, independent reproduction, external review of specific claims, harder repeated missions, and adversarial red-team results.

## What would weaken the claim

Unsupported claims, unreplayable dockets, broken route artifacts, hidden external calls, public/private data boundary failures, wallet or fund movement in public demos, claim-boundary drift, or validator rubber-stamping would weaken the institutional claim.
