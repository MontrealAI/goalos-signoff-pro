Claim maturity

Claims should earn status.

v30 exposes a claim-maturity lattice so projects can distinguish raw assertions from accepted, receipted, reviewable proof.

0unsubmittedA claim exists but no proof package has been submitted.
1submittedEvidence has been provided but not yet validated.
2internally-checkedThe project has run completeness and integrity checks.
3review-readyThe proof package is structured enough for external review.
4independently-replayedA qualified independent party can reproduce or inspect the claim path.
5acceptedHuman/governance authority has accepted the claim within stated boundaries.
6anchored-or-receiptedA signed receipt, public hash, or optional anchor records the decision.
7chronicle-admittedThe accepted proof may influence future missions, reputation, or reusable capability.

Mandate clauses

Language stakeholders can require.

These clauses convert the research into practical due-diligence and governance requirements for blockchain projects.

No Proof Package, No Settlement Readiness

A milestone, grant, upgrade, audit remediation, AI-agent output, or treasury payment is not settlement-ready until the required proof package has been submitted, reviewed, and accepted.

Replay Before Reliance

Any material technical claim should provide a replay path, reproduction packet, or independent review route before it is used to justify governance, payment, or launch.

Human Authority Remains Explicit

Automation may prepare evidence and recommend decisions, but high-impact acceptance remains bound to a named human or governance authority.

Claim Boundary Disclosure

Every public proof package must state what it proves, what it does not prove, what remains unverified, and what risk remains.

Public-Safe Publication

Public demos and public dockets must not request personal/confidential data, use wallet integrations, move funds, or imply production settlement authority unless separately authorized.
