Proof package requirement

What every serious blockchain claim should carry.

Not just a tweet, roadmap, dashboard, GitHub commit, whitepaper, audit badge, or community update. A complete, inspectable proof package.

01

Public proof package URL

A single public place to inspect the claim and its evidence.

The claim remains marketing, not diligence-ready.
02

Mission contract

What was promised, by whom, under what acceptance criteria.

No one can tell whether the work was actually complete.
03

Evidence docket

Artifacts, commits, reports, hashes, provenance, and boundaries.

The project has claims, but no structured proof.
04

Replay path

How another reviewer can check the result again.

The work cannot graduate from assertion to reviewable evidence.
05

Validator review

What independent or role-specific review passed, failed, or challenged.

The claim should not become mature or settlement-ready.
06

Risk ledger

Known limitations, unresolved risks, rollback path, and challenge window.

Stakeholders cannot price or govern the remaining risk.
07

Human authority

Who accepted, rejected, or held the claim.

No release, payout, upgrade, or public reliance should occur.
08

Signed receipt

A durable decision record with hash commitments and boundary language.

No serious credibility or settlement readiness.

DAO delegate

Mission contract, evidence docket, validation note, risk ledger, receipt boundary.

Grant committee

Before a payout, did the milestone pass acceptance?

Acceptance criteria, proof mapping, reviewer signoff, payout readiness receipt.

Treasury council

Before funds move, what proves the vendor earned them?

Deliverable evidence, replay notes, authority decision, settlement gate.

Security reviewer

Before closure, can the fix be independently checked?

Finding map, patch evidence, test output, residual risk, challenge window.

Investor or partner

Before diligence passes, what proof supports the project’s material claims?

Claims matrix, proof status, unresolved risks, receipts, governance boundary.

Enterprise procurement

Before approval, does the project meet institutional evidence standards?

RFP proof checklist, responsible owners, audit trail, redaction boundary.

AI-agent operator

Before the agent is paid or reused, what ProofBundle validates the work?

Task trace, tool scope, validator review, signed receipt, reusable capability gate.

Blockchain trust events

When the mandate should apply.

Any time value, governance, reputation, listing support, launch support, public reliance, or AI-agent settlement is requested, proof should come first.

Decision states

Make the status obvious.

A good credibility layer should make it easy for non-experts to understand whether a claim is merely announced, under review, on hold, or receipt-ready.

CLAIM_ONLY

Do not rely yet.

The project made an assertion without a full proof package.

PROOF_REQUESTED

Pause trust until evidence arrives.

Stakeholders have asked for the proof package.

EVIDENCE_SUBMITTED

Review, but do not settle.

Evidence exists, but replay or validation is incomplete.

VALIDATION_HOLD

Hold payout, launch, or vote.

Some proof exists, but risk, challenge, or authority is unresolved.

RECEIPT_READY

The claim is credibility-ready and settlement-ready in the demo.

Mission, evidence, replay, validation, authority, and receipt are aligned.
